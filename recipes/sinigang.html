Sinigang Recipe

Back to Homepage

Sinigang is a sour soup native and famous in the Philippines. Main ingredient is pork but you can also use shrimp, beef, or fish.

This is really good paired with rice! Let me show you how to make Sinigang!

Ingredients:

  • Pork Belly
  • String Beans
  • Young Tamarind
  • Eggplant
  • Okra
  • Daikon radish
  • Water Spinach
  • Long pepper
  • Onion
  • Fish sauce
  • Tomato
  • Grounded black pepper
    Tips:
  • Pork Belly - 2 lbs. of pork belly for this recipe. Aside from using pork, you may use beef, chicken, fish, or shrimp.
  • Young Tamarind - Unripe tamarind. It has a sour taste and is the traditional souring agent for this dish. You can also use Knorr Sinigang Tamarind if Tamarind is not availale.
  • Water Spinach - Make sure to wash this thoroughly before using.
  • String beans - Green beans (Baguio beans) can be used as an alternative.
  • Eggplant - Slice the eggplants diagonally or roll-cut it.
  • Long green pepper - this is a must have. You can substitute this with either Jalapeno or Serrano pepper.

How to make it?

Steps in how to make Sinigang:

  1. Extract the flavors from the Tamarind (If you don't have Tamarind, skip this step)
  2. Boil the Tamarind Broth (or Knorr Sinigang Tamarind) and cook the meat until tender.
  3. Add the vegetables sequentially
  4. Season it and add the remaining ingredients
  5. Serve hot. Share and enjoy! Don't forget the rice!
